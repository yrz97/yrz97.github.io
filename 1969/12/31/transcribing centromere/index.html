Transcribing Centromeres: Noncoding RNAs and Kinetochore Assembly

###Abstract:

  1. CENP-A loading that form the foundation of the kinetochore(Figure 1 A) may be coupled to centromeric transcription.
  2. Centromeric transcripts can bind to kinetochore and help them stabilize or localize(Figure 1 A).
  3. Some centromeres have promoter activity, and many have non-B form DNA that facilitate their transcription

Centromeric RNA Revisited

noncoding RNAs new function: the abstract 1 and 2

Transcription of Centromeres

[epigenetic mark]:since ectopically incorporated cenH3 , as well as tethering of cenH3 to noncentromeric loci,leads to de novo kinetochore assembly at positions free of centromeric repeats

A defining feature of most centromeres: presence of cenH3, which plays the role of epigenetic mark.

Gene transcription is generally, but not entirely, incompatible with centromere function.

transcription and initiating kinetochore relation:

centromeric transcription might be involved in kinetochore initiation by opening the chromatin to allow cenH3 deposition, and that centromeric transcripts might provide a flexible scaffold for targeting or stabilizing kinetochore proteins.

Centromeric transcripts is an ancient and widespread component of centromeric chromatin. Besides, although transcription of centromeres appears to be general , centromere DNA sequences are not conserved,even between closely related species, and therefore centromeric RNAs are likewise not conserved.

centromeric RNA feature:

  1. transcribed by RNA Polymerase II (Pol II), often on both strands
  2. RNAs are sensitive to RNase A and single stranded RNases, but generally not to DNAses, RNaseH, or double-stranded RNAse.
  3. RNAs are transcribed as longer precursor RNAs that are capped and may be polyadenylated and are processed to a variety of smaller sizes that are distinct from siRNAs, and can vary between centromeres in the same cell and even at the same centromere between cell lines or developmental stages.
  4. Transcription and splicing factors, but not new translation, are required for proper assembly of the kinetochore and spindle.

Centromeric transcripts are present at a low level, and higher transcription levels are often detrimental.
[CENP-C]:a fundamental kinetochore protein that directly binds cenH3 nucleosomes, DNA, and RNA. It serves as an assembly platform for other kinetochore proteins.
*[CPC]:chromosomal passenger complex , a mobile complex consisting of the Aurora-B kinase, INCENP, Borealin, and Survivin, which phosphorylates many proteins during mitosis, regulates spindle attachment by sensing tension, correcting attachment errors, and activating the spindle assembly checkpoint.It localizes to the kinetochore and inner centromere at metaphase, then migrates to the spindle midzone at late anaphase, where it regulates cytokinesis and plays a key role in preventing formation of micronuclei *
[CENPC motif]: a conserved motif of~23 amino acid in CENP-C that binds cenH3 nucleosomes. Many vertebrate CENP-Cs have a second variant of this motif (CENPC-v) in their central region, and most non-mammalian KNL2s also have a variant (CENPC-k) that binds cenH3.

RNA and CENP-C

  1. Hela cell: a-satellite RNAs were observed in nucleoli, along with the CENP-C and INCENP(a component of the CPC):
  • the region that binding has partial specificity to a-satellite RNA.
  • besides, this region can also bind DNA, and there are CENPC-v at central region and CENPC-motif that binding CENP-A

The authors proposed a model in which CENP-C and INCENP, and its binding partner Survivin are preassociated with a-satellite ==RNA== in the nucleolus for effective ==localization== and assembly into the mitotic kinetochore.

==???== however, the nucleolar localization of centromere transcripts was not confirmed using HeLa and other human cells. Nucleolar centromeric transcripts were also reported in maize, but not nucleolar CENP-C, leaving the role of a nucleolar assembly pathway unclear.

  1. Maize cell: CENP-C was also found to bind RNA, although without specificity and only binds one CENP-A.

exons 9–12 were found to bind both RNA and DNA. And binding of a small RNA can enhance chromatin DNA binding. Single-stranded DNA, like RNA, also promoted binding to double-stranded DNA. So centromeric ==RNA== can ==stabilize== CENP-C by increasing its binding to DNA, and help it to ==localize== to cenH3 nucleosome.
[KNL2]: you can see the paper in lab folder
3. kinetochore KNL2 in non-mammals like Arabidopsis(it’s similar to CENP-C):

C terminal portion of KNL2 binds both RNA and DNA , with putative DNA-binding domains on either side of the CENPC-k motif (binding cen H3) that are necessary for centromeric ==localization==. Unlike in maize CENP-C, small RNA had little effect on KNL2 binding DNA but longer RNA competed with DNA for binding KNL2. Possibly centromere transcription prior to metaphase dislodges KNL2 from centromeric DNA at the time when it is replaced by CENP-C to build the kinetochore.

Besides, Kinetochore KNL2 can promote cenH3 incorporation during G2 and is present throughout the cell cycle except from metaphase to late anaphase

conclusion: RNA stabilizes the binding of CENP-C and helps tp localzie to DNA and/or chromatin.

RNA and Aurora-B

The immediate phenotypic consequences (like improper spindle attachment and unaligned chromosomes,micronuclei ) of depleting centromeric RNA are primarily due to improper regulation of Aurora-B and the CPC, with perhaps more minor contributions from destabilizing CENP-C.

Centromeric RNA is necessary to localize and activate Aurora-B(perhaps inducing allosteric changes on binding) at the inner centromere and that elsewhere it may be activated by other RNAs so that it has high avtivity. Borealin also bound RNA
[H2AT120p]:histone H2A that was phosphorylated on T120 by the kinetochore kinase Bub1

Mitotic Transcription and Shugosin1

Pol II was active at the active kinetochore and inner centromere during mitosis and early G1.

H2AT120p is essential to localize Pol II and centromeric transcripts to mitotic kinetochores H3,and then Pol II is recruited to inner centromere at mitosis, but centromeric transcription may occur independent of Bub1 during interphase, however, it is not sufficient, since ectopic GFP-Bub1 on chromosome arms did not accumulate Pol II.Besides, a-satellite was also transcribed from pericentromeres prior to mitosis, predominantly by Pol I.
[H3T3p]: H3 is phosphorylated by haspin, a kinase enriched at the inner centromere
[HJURP]:the dedicated CENP-A chaperone

localization process: Sgo1 and transcripts localizes initially to the kinetochore, where it binds to H2AT120p.Then mitotic centromeric transcripts compete with and release Sgo1 from H2AT120p at the kinetochore, allowing it to move with Pol II to the inner centromere.

Sgo1 also bound to the CPC subunit Borealin, so CPC ‘tags along’ with Sgo1 to move from the kinetochore to the inner centromere, where Sgo1 binds to cohesin and Survivin binds H3T3p.

==???== While this tag-along model is attractive, the roles of centromeric RNAs and transcription in Sgo1 and Aurora-B localization remain to be clarified.
####Transcription and CENP-A Loading
active hitone marks ( like H3K4me2 ) promotes transcription, transcriptions facilitate HJURP recruitment and CENP-A loading.

in Hela cell: RNA associates with soluble HJURP and CENP-A in a preassembly complex:Figure 3A

Individual human a-satellite monomers may be only 50–70% identical and are arranged in higher order repeat arrays that are specific for different chromosomes.Transcripts from specific arrays colocalized only with the corresponding DNA array (‘cis-acting’), indicating that the transcripts do not act in trans at other centromeres. So, depleting RNA using array specific siRNAs led to reduction of CENP-A and CENP-C only at the targeted array and impaired new CENP-A loading, which implies that centromere RNAs play a causal role in the loading or stabilization of CENP-A.

RNAs localized active centromere arrays at CENP-A and CENP-C, but localized both at CENP-B.

conclusion: transcription or transcripts are required for HJURP recruitment and efficient CENP-A loading at human centromeres, neocentromeres,and HACs, and suggest that the primary sequences of the transcripts may be irrelevant.

two population CENP-A: differ in their stability to salt
using Chip:
1.low salt: smaller fragment: incomplete complex(no CENP-B box)
2.high salt: larger fragmemt: full inner kinetochore complex stabilized by CENP-B(because it’s unstable in high salt,so it need to be stabilized by other protein)
[CAL1]:CENP-A chaperone
Because transcription was required for stable salt-resistant incorporation of CENP-A and CAL1, when transcription was inhibited, CENP-A still localized to centromere but was removed by high salt extraction, whereas CENP-C was chromatin-associated in both conditions.

in Drosophila S2 cells: so CAL1 and CENP-A associate with chromatin-bound CENP-C before CENP-A is stably incorporated in the wake of transcription :Figure 3B
[FACT]:general histone chaperone,can promote transcription,its depletion will affect new CENP-A loading, but had little effect on retention of existing CENP-A
CAL1 recruits FACT and Pol II to centromeres, where they facilitate CENP-A loading,perhaps by dislodging H3 nucleosomes and replacing them with CENP-A nucleosomes.

Besides, HJURP acts through a FACT-based mechanism similarly to CAL1. Similarly, in budding yeast, FACT binds to the E3 ubiquitin ligase Psh1 to target misincorporated Cse4 in euchromatin for degradation
[Cse4]:CenH3

Acetylation in H3K9 and H4 (which is histone modification) facilitates transcription, so that promotes denovo CENP-A deposition.

Promoters in Centromeres

The centromeres of many organisms contained numerous promoters, and can initiate centromeric transcripts.

Different portions of the central domains were not equivalent in their ability to assemble CENP-A chromatin, and portions with lower transcriptional activity were more effective than those with higher activity. Stalled polymerases make a favorable chromatin environment for CENP-A loading, perhaps through promoting remodeling or eviction of H3 nucleosomes. In vitro CENP-A nucleosomes themselves present a greater barrier to transcription than H3 nucleosomes and might promote their own recruitment.
[CRM]:Cis-regulatory modules are DNA sequence elements that have transcriptional regulatory activity. Some CRMs are promoter sequences which bind the basal transcription machinery, consisting of RNA polymerase II and many cofactors, and determine where transcription of each gene is initiated
[cruciform extrusion]: that is the form of cruciform
[dyad symmetries]:a palindrome-like region of a DNA molecule with two adjacent or nearly adjacent sequences that are reverse complements, with the potential to form a cruciform structure.

Non-B DNA has been frequently predicted to form at satellite centromeres in many eukaryotes. A form of non-B DNA is cruciform extrusion, promoted by short dyad symmetries( which is also widespread at centromeres ).Besides, non-proliferating (‘resting’) B cells showed reduced levels of centromeric non-B DNA, because proliferation induces cruciform extrusion for ‘seeding’ centromeres.

Another form of non-B DNA is R loops, which form at transcribing centromeres from RNA–DNA hybrids, and aid Aurora B activation.

Two hypotheses of role of non-B DNA at centromeres

  1. four-way junctions of cruciforms are bound by Holliday junction binding activity of HJURP, whereupon HJURP would load CENP-A/H4.

  2. non-B DNA might result from transcriptional initiation, where melting of DNA is required for engagement of Pol II, or result from Pol II elongation, which moves the denaturation bubble forward.

In both cases the enigmatic CENP-B sequence-specific DNA binding protein likely plays a role. Given the enrichment of non-B form DNA at centromeres throughout ==the eukaryotic domain==, it seems likely that this feature of centromeres can provide a basis for centromere specification despite the lack of primary sequence conservation.
